What is Carrom?

Carrom is a popular tabletop game of Indian origin. It is played on a square board with pockets at each corner, where players use a striker disk to flick lighter object disks (carrom men) into the pockets.

History of Carrom

Carrom is believed to have originated in India around the 18th century. It gained popularity in the Indian subcontinent and spread to other parts of Asia and beyond. The game has seen many international championships and is a favorite in households and clubs.

How to Play Carrom

Carrom is played on a wooden board with a smooth surface. Players use a striker to hit and pocket carrom men, aiming to pocket their own color of pieces while avoiding the opponent's pieces. The game requires skill and precision to control the striker and carrom men.

Rules of Carrom

Key rules of Carrom include:

1. Each player has to pocket all their carrom men before the opponent.

2. The Queen, the red piece, must be pocketed and covered by another piece of the player's color.

3. If a player pockets the striker, they incur a penalty, and a piece is returned to the board.

4. The first player to pocket all their pieces, including covering the Queen, wins the game.
