What is Hockey?

Hockey is a fast-paced team sport played on ice, where the objective is to score goals by hitting a puck into the opposing team's net using a hockey stick. It is a popular sport in many countries, particularly in Canada, the United States, and Northern Europe.

History of Hockey

Hockey has ancient origins, with various forms of stick-and-ball games played across different cultures. Modern ice hockey as we know it today originated in Canada in the late 19th century. The first organized indoor game was played in Montreal in 1875, and the sport quickly spread to other countries.

How to Play Hockey

Hockey is played on an ice rink, with two teams of six players each, including a goaltender. Players use their sticks to pass, shoot, and control the puck, aiming to score goals while preventing the opposing team from doing the same. The game is known for its physicality, speed, and skill.

Rules of Hockey

Hockey has a comprehensive set of rules that govern how the game is played. Key rules include offside, icing, penalties, and the duration of the game.

Offside rule: A player is offside if they enter the attacking zone before the puck does.

Icing: Icing occurs when a player shoots the puck across both the center red line and the opposing team's goal line without it being touched.

Penalties: Penalties are assessed for infractions such as tripping, hooking, and high-sticking, resulting in a player being sent to the penalty box for a designated time.

Duration of the game: A standard hockey game consists of three periods, each lasting 20 minutes, with intermissions between periods.
