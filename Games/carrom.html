What is Carrom?

Carrom is a family of tabletop games sharing a similarity in that their mechanics lie somewhere between billiards and table shuffleboard. It is believed to have originated in India, possibly as far back as the 18th century.

History of Carrom

The game of carrom originated in India, possibly as far back as the 18th century. It is believed to have evolved from an Indian game called "karam." Carrom is played by people of all ages across the Indian subcontinent and beyond.

How to Play Carrom

Carrom is played on a square board with a pocket in each corner. The goal is to sink the carrom men (wooden disks) into the pockets using the striker, a larger piece. Players take turns flicking the striker with their fingers, aiming to hit the carrom men and score points.
