What is Ludo?

Ludo is a board game for two to four players, in which the players race their four tokens from start to finish according to the rolls of a single die. It is derived from the Indian game Pachisi, but simpler.

History of Ludo

The game of Ludo is derived from the Indian game Pachisi, which was created in the 6th century. The earliest evidence of this game in India is the depiction of boards on the caves of Ellora. Ludo was introduced to the United Kingdom as "Ludo" in 1896.

How to Play Ludo

Ludo is played on a square board with a cross-shaped game track, with each arm of the cross consisting of three columns of squares. Players roll a single die to move their tokens around the board, aiming to move all their tokens to the home column before their opponents.

Rules of Ludo

The basic rules of Ludo include:

1. Rolling a six allows a player to bring a new token into play.

2. Players take turns rolling the die and moving their tokens based on the die's outcome.

3. Tokens can capture opponents' tokens by landing on the same square, sending the captured token back to its starting area.

4. The goal is to move all of one's tokens to the home column first to win the game.
