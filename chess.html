What is Chess?

Chess is a strategic board game played between two players. It is known for its deep strategic elements and is one of the most popular board games worldwide, enjoyed by millions of players of all ages.

History of Chess

Chess originated in northern India in the 6th century and spread to Persia. When the Arabs conquered Persia, the game was taken up by the Muslim world and subsequently spread to Southern Europe. The game evolved to its current form in Europe during the 15th century.

How to Play Chess

Chess is played on an 8x8 grid called a chessboard. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. The objective is to checkmate the opponent's king, meaning it is under attack and has no legal move to escape.

Rules of Chess

Chess has a set of rules that govern how each piece moves and how the game is played.

Piece movements: Each type of piece has its own way of moving on the board. For example, rooks move in straight lines, bishops move diagonally, and knights move in an L-shape.

Check and checkmate: A king is in check if it is under threat of capture. Checkmate occurs when the king is in check and cannot make any legal move to escape.

Castling: A special move that involves the king and one of the rooks, allowing them to move simultaneously under certain conditions to improve the king's safety.

En passant: A special pawn capture that can occur immediately after a pawn moves two squares forward from its starting position and lands beside an opponent's pawn.

Promotion: When a pawn reaches the opposite end of the board, it can be promoted to any other piece, usually a queen.
